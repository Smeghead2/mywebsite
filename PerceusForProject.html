Perceus was born of a mortal mother and Zeus. His grandfather, king of a city, locked him and his mother in a wooden crate and sent the out to sea. They washed up on shore of a nearby town and were taken in by a fisherman who was the brother of the king. The king takes a special interest in Perseus's mother, and Perseus blocks him at every chance.

The king holds a banquet, requesting horses as a gift. Perseus, being poor, asks the king to name another gift, and he would not refuse. The king, wanting Perseus dead, tells him to go find and slay the mortal gorgon, Medusa, whose gaze can turn one to stone. So Perseus goes to the three wise old ladies in the north to find the garden of the Hesperides, who will tell him where to find Medusa

Perseus holds the shared eye of the ladies ranson until they tell him where to find the garden. Once they told him, he returned the eye and left. At the garden, Zeus bestows Perseus with the sharpest sword, Athena gives him a polished, reflective mirror, and Hermes gives the boy a pair of winged shoes and a magic bag. So Perseus flies to the location of Medusa, after being told where by the Hesperides, and finds the gorgon asleep

Perseus sneaks up backwards, using the mirror to see, and slices the head clean off the gorgon. Perseus puts the head into the bag, and flies off. On his way home, he finds the princess Andromeda chained to a rock by the sea. He asks her why she is there, to which she says she is sacrifice to the horrible sea monster. Perseus waits for the monster to arise and uses the head of Medusa to turn it to stone.

Perseus cuts Andromeda's chains and takes her home with him. Once in front of the king, he plops the head down, and the king turns to stone. Perseus marries Andromeda, his mother marries the fisherman, and the myth ends happy.
